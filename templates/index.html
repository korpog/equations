Solving systems of linear equations

Cramer's rule applies to systems of linear equations which have as many equations as unknowns. $$a_{11}x_{1} + a_{12}x_{2} + ... + a_{1n}x_{n} = b_{1}.$$ $$a_{21}x_{1} + a_{22}x_{2} + ... + a_{2n}x_{n} = b_{2}.$$ $$....$$ $$a_{n1}x_{1} + a_{n2}x_{2} + ... + a_{nn}x_{n} = b_{n}.$$ Coefficients of every equation are being represented in a square matrix. The determinant of this matrix can be then used for finding solutions in the form of $$x_{i} = \frac{det(A_{i})}{det(A)}.$$ where \(A_{i}\) is the matrix \(A\) with \(i\)-th column replaced with values from the column vector \(b\).
